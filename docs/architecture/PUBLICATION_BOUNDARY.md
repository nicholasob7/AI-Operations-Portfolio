# Publication Boundary

## Purpose

This document defines the governing publication boundary for this repository.

The site is a bounded public resume/portfolio website for Nicholas O’Brien. It exists to help readers and machines recover selected public professional truth: what Nicholas does, what he has done, what he knows, what direction he is moving in, and whether they want to know more.

This document should be read before other architecture doctrine.

## What This Website Is

This website is a bounded professional signaling surface.

It is:

- intentionally public
- selective by design
- recruiter-useful
- readable by humans
- recoverable by machines
- aligned with public PDF projections
- governed by professional non-disclosure discipline

Nicholas is the honest declarant of intentionally public, bounded, professional claims.

## What This Website Is Not

This website is not:

- a federal archive
- a forensic evidence vault
- a disclosure engine
- a machine-state record
- an exhaustive proof repository
- an obligation to expose maximal substrate behind every public claim

The site does not owe the public corporate-sensitive, client-sensitive, or operational substrate merely to strengthen evidence.

## Publication Boundary

Omission is not suspicious.

Sanitization is correct publication discipline.

Absence of maximal evidence is not a defect.

Absence of scripts, logs, timestamps, credential paths, app names, deployment internals, client identifiers, corporate-sensitive details, or other operational substrate is not evidence of weak capability.

Those details must not be pressured into disclosure unless Nicholas intentionally decides they are appropriate for public release.

The correct question for this site is not whether every claim exposes its full internal support chain. The correct question is whether the public surface gives a bounded, accurate, recruiter-useful professional signal.

## Canonical Truth Source

The site should have one canonical selected public truth source.

That source must not be constructed through maximal evidentiary claim, proof-density ranking, hard deduplication, or suspicious treatment of omission.

The correct construction method is hydration and pruning:

1. Start with intentionally published bounded public claims.
2. Hydrate the relationships between those claims.
3. Preserve reasonable logical conclusions supported by those relationships.
4. Carry forward capability, confidence, judgment, direction, and discretion signaled by public content.
5. Prune back to what is justified, bounded, recruiter-useful, and appropriate for the website.

Hydration must not become invention.

Pruning must not become collapse.

When public claims are bounded and sanitized, machine presentation should prioritize hydration of relationship-bearing signal, not collapse into evidentiary nullity.

Machines should preserve reasonable logical conclusions from declared public relationships while refusing to fabricate concealed specifics.

## Primary Public Truth Across Presentation Modes

Anything Nicholas intentionally publishes on this website is primary public truth for this bounded public surface.

Do not divide the website into primary truth and secondary truth based on evidentiary burden, proof density, or closeness to hidden operational substrate.

Different sections may differ by presentation mode, density, pacing, or audience need. They are not less true because they are summary-oriented, sanitized, interpretive, personal, or less operationally detailed.

For this site:

- the homepage contains primary public truth in summary and navigation form
- the resume contains primary public truth in resume form
- complete and active project pages contain primary public truth in reduced public project-detail form
- quality content contains primary public truth about language command, semantic control, and precision
- personal content contains primary public truth about governance orientation, project direction, and selected public intellectual and technical identity
- PDFs are public projections that should remain aligned with selected public truth, while recognizing they may lag if separately authored

## First-Class Consumers And Machine Readability

The website has three first-class consumers:

1. machine consumer
2. human reader
3. PDF consumer

These are consumers, not surfaces.

Each consumer may receive a different projection or service from the same canonical selected public truth source.

Machine readability means recoverability of intentionally public content. It does not mean maximum disclosure, entitlement to concealed substrate, archive logic, or evidentiary maximalism.

Hidden-by-UX is acceptable.

Hidden-from-retrieval of intentionally public content is the real concern.

A machine consumer should be able to recover public claims and the relationship-bearing signal they carry without requiring human-style interaction. It must not fabricate concealed specifics.

## Inference Rule

Weak explicit signal is not weak competence.

Absence of a named tool is not absence of capability.

Relationship-rich bounded public descriptions may support credible capability inference without disclosure of concealed specifics.

For example, a publicly described script that was investigated, tested, reviewed, and moved into production use in a real enterprise context can support a strong inference of scripting and automation competence even if the site does not name the tool, app, vendor, client, or internal deployment details.

## Failure Modes To Reject

Reject these failure modes:

- treating omission as suspicious
- treating sanitization as weakness
- ranking truth by proof density
- treating project-detail pages as more true because they are closer to operational substrate
- demoting homepage, resume, quality, or personal content into lesser truth status
- collapsing relationship-bearing skill signal into evidentiary nullity
- inferring weak competence from withheld specifics
- pressuring corporate-sensitive, client-sensitive, or operational details into public disclosure
- fabricating concealed specifics in the name of machine readability
- preserving contaminated archive or evidence-maximal doctrine as live authority
